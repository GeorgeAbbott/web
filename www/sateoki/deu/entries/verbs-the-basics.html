Verbs: The Basics

The verb is the most integral part of the sentence in any language - it communicates just what is actually being done. And so to start off, it is vital we learn about the verb in German before we begin doing anything else.

Vocabulary

German Word English Meaning
ich I
du you
er he
sie she
es it

Whenever you go to look up a verb in German, you will see that it ends in en. This might seem odd coming from English, but really the en at the end of a verb can be thought of like the word to that we always append to verbs - it marks the infinitive, the uninflected form of the word when we are just using it in a dictionary. To think is denken, to eat is essen.

(Even at this early stage you may notice how similar English and German words are; for more information on this please consult the later chapter about sounds correspondences between English and German - as it turns out, they are so similar that given knowledge of the word in one language and just a couple of rules, you can easily figure out what the word will be in the other language!)

A core part of German verbs is conjugation. We have this in English as well - I eat, but he eats. In German though, verbs conjugate a bit further.
