Proto-Indo-European Grammar

The common ancestor of hundreds of other languages, including mine own, is Proto-Indo-European. It is reconstructed language which is not attested in anything other that the similarity between modern day languages across Europe and India. It is a highly interesting language...

but: it is very difficult to understand.

Whenever I read about PIE, I am bombarded by terms like proterokinetic, whatever that is meant to mean, and I do not know of any easy explanations of Indo-European grammar in a way that could say, actually help me to learn (what we know of) it. I just want an easy, point by point explanation.

This is the reason I have created this subdomain. I wish to create a easy guide to PIE, by doing the academic reading myself (to help me get to a better understanding of it; my PIE knowledge is woefully lacking) and creating the guide to PIE myself. I will link any sources I use in the creation of this site.

Please bear with me for now - this site is work in progress, and sadly it might take a while for me to update this.
