Kinship in English

This is a brief entry on various kinship words (words for family, e.g. mother, father, brother, sister) in English. I will make another similar entry on spousal terms, but across languages instead, comparing how they are derived.

The Basic Kinship Terms

It pains me to say, but the etymologies for basic kinship terms like mother, father, brother, sister, son and daughter are very simple. They have survived thousands of years from Proto-Indo-European with no change in meaning (because the meaning is so simple and has no real reason to change). Because of this, if I am not careful I will just find myself going: mother is from PIE méh₂tēr, which means mother, and so on and so forth for all other kinship words. Because of this I will instead discuss possible etymologies for these words in PIE, not just in English.
