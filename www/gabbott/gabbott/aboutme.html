About Me

This page gives some information about myself, and my hobbies and interests.

Programming

I first got interested in programming as a young teen, when I discovered cmd.exe on Windows, and realized that I could run commands on it to make the computer do cool things. I would probably spend most of my time running echo and taskkill -F explorer.exe - I would kill explorer.exe, bring up a terminal and make it feel like I was using MS-DOS.

Soon after in year 7 we had computer science classes and were introduced to Scratch (which I found a bit dull) and Python, which was incredible to me. I had been used to writing blocks of code in Batch script using GOTOs, and then using more GOTOs to get me back to where I was, so even the idea of a function was a massive help.

I kept on using Python all throughout my life and even up to A levels, but even at that point I was looking into other languages. I started looking at C# for work experience and found it quite enjoyable and began working with it where I could.

In the past year or so I have gotten into Rust and started working a little more with C++ as well. Both of these languages seem to be interesting and Rust is very enjoyable to work with. C++ seems like a bit of a mess, but I enjoy the syntax and where the language is going.

By and large, software development has long since been one of my main interests, alongside languages below.

Languages

Also in my teen years I began studying German. I have family in Germany, but really as I never see them I am not really sure what the reason I started learning was.

Either way, I found German very interesting and took to it quite well. What I particularly enjoy is learning about grammar and syntax, less so vocabulary.

Just after my GCSEs, I decided I wanted to learn another language as I had gotten to the point where my German was fairly good (my comprehension at least) and so I decided I would learn Norwegian and Polish. I spent the most part of the two years of sixth form learning these languages, but eventually gave up the ghost as they just weren't grabbing me - Norwegian was too easy and the grammar was rather boring, and I had already learnt everything interesting in Polish grammar, and was faced with the realisation that to progress now would just be to bruteforce remember irregular forms (such as the numbers). I decided not to bother and so gave these languages up. That said, I still remember a fair bit from both of them and can generally understand a fair bit of Norwegian (not as much Polish unfortunately).

Then during sixth form, to skip out on having to study for exams, I looked about and found Japanese. Japanese seemed very interesting - it was outside the European sprachbund so would have an interesting grammar, it was strictly head-final (I am a big fan of head-final languages), had a decently complex verbal morphology, etc. As well as this, and the main reason I chose Japanese over another language with similar characteristics such as Korean, Mongolian, Turkish, Manchu, etc. was that I am a fan of anime and so would have a decent amount of media I could watch.

Really now, a couple years on, I think I appreciate Japanese even more than the surface-level view I had of it when I went into learning it. The languages feels incredibly expressive to me in a way that, much as I do appreciate German, it simply isn't, and I enjoy the sound of Japanese as well (not as beautiful-sounding as Norwegian, but close).
