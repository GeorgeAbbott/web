The Progress of a Crime Review

This is my first real venture into crime stories of this kind, which I decided to pick up at Waterstones a year or two ago. The only other crime book I've ever really read apart from this is Nisioisin's Decapitation, which was very good (if after the first 100 odd pages which are dull), so I was hoping this was going to be as good.

Sadly it wasn't.

I don't have a massive amount to say about this as a book though, as it was just uninteresting. Not so bad it's worth talking about, just dull.

I might try and expand with some detail at some point, but that is about all I've gotten from this read. I bought some others at the same time, e.g. Fell Murder, Excellent Intentions, The Murder of my Aunt and Deep Waters, all a part of the same set of murder mystery books which are British Library Classics. I hope when I get around to reading these they are a bit more interesting.

Too people-focussed?

I wonder if I might not have liked it because it was too focussed on people, and not the actual murder mystery part. And all the sort of romance between the detective and whatever her name was that woman, it all really just seemed to bore me. I think I would have preferred it to focus on the actual mystery (though, really, there wasn't much of a mystery. It's probably not murder mystery genre, just crime)

Nice Words

One thing to give the book props for was the many nice words, a lot of which I didn't know. It's always nice to read a book and find you are learning new words you could use.

Some examples:

  • pg. 129: inveterate, acidulous
  • pg. 131: desultory (knew, but rarely see)
  • pg. 132: depositions
  • pg. 133: insouciance (knew, but rarely see and is a cool word)
  • pg. 163: unctuous mock-sorrow
  • pg. 178: bonhomous
  • pg. 191: histrionic
  • pg. 205: sententious

    • Odd Story at the end

    There was a brief little story at the end of the book, entitled Tigers in Subtopia. Frankly, it was a bit weird, and I am not really sure what to get from it. It was about a hysterical posh woman convinced that lowerclass boys have kidnapped her son, and so the husband takes the law into his own hands, and spanks the boy he believes responsible, only to find his son has just gone to a party and his wife somehow... forgot. Not really sure what the moral is.
