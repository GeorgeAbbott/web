Refraining from Oil and Sugar

I am thinking of refraining from oil and sugar going forwards.

Why?

Aside from the obvious health problems with oil and sugar (we are of course talking table sugar here, not natural sugars in fruit and the like) you could also argue that sugar is procured immorally. Historically that was of course the case, and I suspect (granted without evidence) that the Fairtrade certification used to mark “moral” products are just meaningless badges put over the product to make it appealing to cityites who want to feel good, like the Red Lion, or approval from the RSPCA, and it is still very bad today.

So here, I will need to think about all the foods I eat of an everyday basis which has sugar, or oil in it, and try to think of alternatives.

Breakfast

There are three main types of foods people (and myself) eat for breakfast: porridge, toast, and cereals.

For cereals, it's difficult to find completely sugar-free, but wheat biscuits, e.g. Weetabix, are fairly good. Apart from that, hard to find.

For toast, if the bread is bought from a shop, it probably has to go. Homemade bread is probably fine though, as it generally doesn't need much sugar. For a topping, use (homemade) jam, or perhaps peanut butter (I guess this is oil, but it's better).

For porridge: perfect. This is probably the best breakfast anyways. Just make sure the milk you have doesn't have sugar added! I recommend cinnamon with it instead of sugar.

Dinner

For a default dinner, I tend to have a kind of meat-and-two-veg meal like most people. As for the veg, simply don't cook it in oil. If making mash (a mainstay), don't add butter (if vegan butter). You can still add some milk though.

For the meat, well, I don't eat meat, but for the fake meat that I eat, there are ways around it. The burgers and the like from the shops are going to have to go. So, how do I get my protein, he worries?

Well, as for real meat there is a lot else you are missing out on, but fake meat as I eat is basically tofu. So just eat tofu. You can cook it in any old way. Apart from that, supplement it with other things like lentils. For instance, cook up some lentils and freeze it.

Lunches

For lunches, potentially some beans (Heinz does some sugar-free ones) and a baked potato. Or, some soup. These kinds of things.

Snacks

Fruit is a good bet.

Some common foods that now seem missed

  • Jam: it is best to make your own, though it is expensive.. I haven't tried it but apparently you can use apples/flaxseed/chia seed/potato as a pectin replacement, and do without the sugar (or use artificial sugars, but not sure how I feel about that.)
  • Vegan Butter: these are just pure oil basically so have to go. I am not sure on a good replacement, and probably best just not to eat it.
