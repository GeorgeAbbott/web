First Foray Into Custom ROMs and Rooting

A month or two ago I made the decision to install a custom ROM onto my S9+ that I had bought a couple years ago. For some reason I made the decision back then to buy the newest, most expensive phone, I think just to try out what that peak-of-technology consumerism felt like. I don't think I got much out of it.

The decision was based on a few things, such as wanting to get the newer version of Android (as my phone was out of its update period) and also wanting to get rid of all the Samsung and Google spying that was on my old phone, and have something that just had nice, clean, FOSS software on it and none of the rest of the junk that is on most smartphones nowadays.

I installed LineageOS onto an old S4 Mini that I had lying around as a test, and was pleasantly surprised just how easy it was. Really, the only steps were to unlock the bootloader (in the S4 Mini it was already unlocked), flash a recovery, flash the ROM and bang. Only a few commands and it was up and running. I had heard before that rooting involved a chance of bricking the phone, but really it was such an easy process I was not sure where the worry was.

I then decided I would install LineageOS (although debated a bit about whether /e/OS might be better, but I am happy with my choice now) onto the S9, after seeing how easy it was, and got to doing it after making sure I wouldn't lose access to any accounts, wouldn't lose my 2FA tokens, etc. by porting everything I had onto the S4. It took maybe 20 minutes?

I wanted to install microG on this, as the ROM did not come with it by default, but realised that would involve rooting, using Xposed, and all of those kinds of things. I did not really know and left it, getting on with just using the new OS normally without microG, and then this Christmas I decided to finally root and install microG.

It took a little bit more effort and research than installing the ROM, but really it was easy as well. All you have to do is grab the boot.img, flash it through Magisk, reinstall the boot image (normally with fastboot flash BOOT boot.img, but that did not work here, as Samsung devices do not have fastboot, so you use Odin on Windows or heimdall on Linux (so I just had to run heimdall flash --BOOT boot.img instead), and then the rooting is complete. After that is just to install Riru and LSPosed through Magisk (it took me a good while wondering why EdXPosed was not working, before realising that newer versions of Riru only work with LSPosed not EdXPosed), install the microG also through Magisk, and install the FakeGApps fork by whew-inc (the version in the F-Droid store does not work AFAICR).

And that's it! It took me about half a day to get rooting and microG to work after I got around to it, but the full process of going from stock ROM to rooted, LineageOS with microG can be done probably in less than an hour if you know what you are doing, and doesn't take more than a day or so when you don't (like me).

I want to at some point try see if I can fork CalyxOS onto the S9, but that is a massive task I imagine. For now, the phone works, and I have learnt a thing or two about rooting. I will probably not get any new phones until this one irreparably breaks, but I am happy with what I have - a decent phone that does not track me for ads or have any software bloat like most phones come with, but nice and clean with few applications, no bloat, and as fast as the day it came out the box.

Ultimately I think more people should do this, especially considering how easy it is, and the advantages to it in terms of privacy and degoogling. I will recommend it to my family when they get new phones.
