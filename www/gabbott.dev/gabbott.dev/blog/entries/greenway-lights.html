Lights along the Greenway

I went along the greenway today, and saw that it was closed, due to them putting lights along it. I initially was a bit disturbed by it - I can understand wanting to make it lighter, but at the same time, it is good that the greenway is dark at night - the entire rest of the town is lit up extremely bright (the light pollution here is, like most cities, terrible). So the dark greenway gives foxes, bats, and other nocturnal animals some actual darkness, and generally helps what little nature we have in this city.

Doing a little research on it, it seems the lights are going to be "smart", or basically, will have a sensor, and will only light up when people are walking up to it. They are also solar powered, so they don't use any electricity (though, the lithium, etc. in the battery more than offsets any good thoughts environmentally, so it's not like it's going to be better as carbon goes.) I think this is a good compromise though - it appeases the fears of crime in the darkness, but also won't make the already terrible light pollution situation even worse. That said, it will depend on implementation, so let's wait and see.

Well done, Southend Council. I think this is the first time I've ever been even slightly impressed by them.
