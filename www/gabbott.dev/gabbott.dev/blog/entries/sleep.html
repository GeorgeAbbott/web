Sleep

One thing I'm realising is that I can't buy time from sleep. It may seem obvious, but it still hasn't fully sunk in from me yet. I can only trade time.

For instance, last night, I stayed up until late, as I had work I wanted to get done on the laptop and the first bit of sorting out photos I took at Hope (there are over 500, and it will take a long time).

So I stayed up until one. I tried to get up at six the next morning as I wanted to go to the allotment - no luck. I woke up feeling absolutely tired, and realised I just needed to keep on sleeping. I eventually woke up at about twenty past eight.

So, instead of going to bed at ten or eleven like would be sensible, I stayed up until one. But then, I woke up in the morning, had to sleep an extra two hours - offsetting the two or so hours I had gained, and feeling terrible for it. I gained no time, I just felt worse.

That is the nature of the trade. I can buy hours, but they will be reaped back - and the cost is, that I feel terrible for it. I pay a premium just to use my hours sooner rather than later.

Whilst I don't imagine a perfectly consistent bedtime is good either, in its restricting regularity, I will need to learn the lesson of not trying to borrow time - it must be paid back, and with a high interest rate.
