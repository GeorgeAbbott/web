Plans for the Allotment in 2024

To set out my plans for the allotment in 2024 - this will be a working document and so will get changed over time.

The Beds

I (will) have at the allotment -

  • berry bed
  • failed parsnip and failed turnips patches
  • leek bed
  • tarp bed
  • three bees
  • long bed
  • the two circle beds
  • six planters
  • flower bed
  • various hanging baskets around the place

Of these, the circle beds and the flower bed (obviously) will be dedicated to growing (mostly) ornamental flowers, and the rest can be dedicated to crops.

Crops to Grow

  • Celeriac - never tried it, would like to.
  • Kohlrabi - never tried it, would like to.
  • Strawberries - lots of them, in the hanging baskets
  • Rhubarb - we have a plant at home, but I wouldn't mind a second one.
  • Tomatoes - Golden Sunrise definitely. Maybe some black tomatoes as well.
  • Leeks - a personal favourite.
  • Globe and Jerusalem artichokes - never tried them.
  • Turnips - I'll try again. But twice now my turnips have failed to germinate. Apparently they're "easy".
  • Parsnips - see turnips.
  • Carrots - maybe try Purple Dragon again. Maybe Blanche a Collet Vert as well, as well as more traditional orange carrots, e.g. Nantes. They taste disgusting when bought from the shops now, so I would want some of my own.
  • Radishes - only for successional sowing, I don't actually like them.

Flowers I want to grow

  • Borage - very pretty and bees like it apparently. Will be good to try.
  • Calendula - again, they should self-seed themselves.
  • Zinnia - both Purple Prince again and Old Mexico.
  • Gaillardia - I sowed these twice this year and none of them came up. I will try again, I do want to get to see them.
  • Rudbeckias - they're pretty.
  • Amaranthus - Love Lies Bleeding of course, but also Celosia. I will see if I can save any seed from the Celosia I have this year.
  • Nasturtiums - definitely. They taste good too!
  • Daffodils - for borders and the like.
  • Hyacinths - Gypsy Queen and Midnight Mystic. Probably in pots.
  • Lillies - I have various lillies on order for next year, and might also get a Pretty Woman or Picasso (I like the freckles on Picasso).
  • Crocus - this might be pretty.
  • Choisya - potentially. It really depends if I have anywhere to put it, as it can't really move from when it is planted. But it would be pretty. I imagine near the pond could be nice.
  • Salvia - I might choose some red and some blue.
  • Hydrangea - if I can. I would like to grow both Blue Danube and Miss Saori. But where will I find the space...? I think I will probably have to give this a miss until I can find a place to put it.
  • Gardenia - perhaps? But I will need to find ericaceous soil.
  • Foxgloves
  • Snowdrops
  • Comfrey - for use as fertiliser.

Flower Bed

The flower bed has a few perennials already in it, namely - an armeria, two fuchsias (Snowcap and Alice Hoffman - are they hardy? will they overwinter?), gaura, lavender, some mint. As well, there are two hollyhock which need to stay there until next year to flower - a nigra, and a Creme de Cassis.

In this bed, I want a gradual progression, from vivid, fiery reds, to oranges and yellows to whites, and then back to blues, along from left to right.

  • Nasturtium - Empress of India

  • Chrysanthemum: already there.

Strawberries

I need to consider where I can put hanging baskets for strawberries, and what varieties then to grow.

I would like to grow both summer-bearing and ever-bearing varieties, so I can get an even mix of strawberries, some in summer and some throughout the year.

Varieties

  • Murano - definitely do NOT grow this. It is the suoermarket variety, and does not taste very good at all.
  • Honeoye - of course.
  • Cambridge Favourite
  • Flamenco - an ever-bearing variety.
  • Toscana - ever-bearing, but note it is an F1. Again, do I want F1s? Of course I won't save seeds from this, so can just keep runners if I want to propagate.

As for placing, I can have two hanging baskets (at least) above the berry bed. One above the leek bed. Several along the path running along three bees and the tarp bed. Several in the sitting area. Some by the pond. Maybe some by the shed. Basically, I am not going to run low on places to put them. I can put them above any bed, so that when I water the hanging basket, it runs off into the bed.

Long Bed

In the long bed, I have the lily (Bright Joy) that will stay there. The leeks, tomatoes, and peppers will not stay after winter. The chilli might overwinter, but I will get rid of some, or move them elsewhere. The strawverries will stay. But that means I have almost the full section of the bed in which to put... whatever I want, except for the little bit with the strawberries.

Towards the end with the lily, I might give it some space. I have chard there at present, and might instead have some leeks, or carrots growing there. Something I can put in a neat orderly way and which grows underground.

Then, after two rows of these carrots or leeks, we can begin tomatoes. I want to have more tomatoes, and will dedicate a large section of the allotment to these tomatoes. I can have two places in which to put the tomatoes to separate out and prevent blight. Hence, the failed turnip patch would be another good place to put them. The tomatoes will stay there the whole year, really.

After the tomatoes, I will still have a bit of space at the end. What do I do with it? Well, some of that will be occupied by the strawberries, but the rest will be able to be used. Perhaps onions when they are to go in, so around March. I will probably try onions from seed if I can.
