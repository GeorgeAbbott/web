Plans for the Allotment in 2024

To set out my plans for the allotment in 2024 - this will be a working document and so will get changed over time.

The Beds

I (will) have at the allotment -

  • berry bed
  • failed parsnip and failed turnips patches
  • leek bed
  • tarp bed
  • three bees
  • long bed
  • the two circle beds
  • six planters
  • flower bed
  • various hanging baskets around the place

Of these, the circle beds and the flower bed (obviously) will be dedicated to growing (mostly) ornamental flowers, and the rest can be dedicated to crops.

Crops to Grow

  • Celeriac - never tried it, would like to.
  • Kohlrabi - never tried it, would like to.
  • Strawberries - lots of them, in the hanging baskets
  • Rhubarb - we have a plant at home, but I wouldn't mind a second one.
  • Tomatoes - Golden Sunrise definitely. Maybe some black tomatoes as well.
  • Leeks - a personal favourite.
  • Globe and Jerusalem artichokes - never tried them.
  • Turnips - I'll try again. But twice now my turnips have failed to germinate. Apparently they're "easy".
  • Parsnips - see turnips.
  • Carrots - maybe try Purple Dragon again. Maybe Blanche a Collet Vert as well, as well as more traditional orange carrots, e.g. Nantes. They taste disgusting when bought from the shops now, so I would want some of my own.
  • Radishes - only for successional sowing, I don't actually like them.

Flowers I want to grow

  • Borage - very pretty and bees like it apparently. Will be good to try.
  • Calendula - again, they should self-seed themselves.
  • Zinnia - both Purple Prince again and Old Mexico.
  • Gaillardia - I sowed these twice this year and none of them came up. I will try again, I do want to get to see them.
  • Rudbeckias - they're pretty.
  • Amaranthus - Love Lies Bleeding of course, but also Celosia. I will see if I can save any seed from the Celosia I have this year.
  • Nasturtiums - definitely. They taste good too!
  • Daffodils - for borders and the like.
  • Hyacinths - Gypsy Queen and Midnight Mystic. Probably in pots.
  • Lillies - I have various lillies on order for next year, and might also get a Pretty Woman or Picasso (I like the freckles on Picasso).
  • Crocus - this might be pretty.
  • Choisya - potentially. It really depends if I have anywhere to put it, as it can't really move from when it is planted. But it would be pretty. I imagine near the pond could be nice.
  • Salvia - I might choose some red and some blue.
  • Hydrangea - if I can. I would like to grow both Blue Danube and Miss Saori. But where will I find the space...? I think I will probably have to give this a miss until I can find a place to put it.
