Convenience

This will only be a quick one.

I wonder, to what extent is inconvenience a bad thing? Is it bad at all? And what inconvenience should be tolerated, and what not?

I am thinking this as I have recently come into need of a lot of cardboard, for covering over the allotment with, and been mulling over just buying it in. For now, I have been collecting cardboard every time I go to the shops, and have been asking people around me to "donate" whatever cardboard they have. So far it has worked out well, and I have a decent amount, but it certainly would have been more convenient to just buy a whole load of cardboard. So is the inconvenience worth the cost?

I suppose there is an argument to be made that I have benefitted from the inconvenience. My attention span might be longer because of it, say. And of course, by recycling other people's cardboard I have found, I have saved it from being chucked away, whilst also saving new cardboard from needing to be produced to that amount that I used. That is a good thing, I guess.

But how far does this apply? Is inconvenience always good? Or is it sometimes bad? What if the effect on the environment wasn't there? Or if any positive effects generally were so small as to be unnoticeable? Would there still be benefit in inconvenience over convenience? Or is a balance necessary - I don't buy all my own clothes, nor produce all my own food, and it would be inconvenient to the point of impossible to do so.

Not sure really. Not looking for answers exactly, just wondering. I would probably side that generally, inconvenience is beneficial. But certainly I do not apply that to all things, and know it is not feasible to do so either. I guess it is similar to whether to use low-level languages and high-level languages, or generally what I wrote in The Middle Way as well.
