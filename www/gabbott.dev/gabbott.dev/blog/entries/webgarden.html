Blog, or webgarden?

It is not uncommon to see people create digital gardens, as opposed to the idea of a blog. A blog is chronological - it is one entry after another, and a focus is put on the date of publishing. There is an expectation - not one that necessarily needs to be upheld, but a tendency at least - that a post won't be edited (except for some extra footnotes, etc.) after the initial publishing.

And thinking about it - is there beauty in a blog? I don't really think so. I have read this, about how the blog broke the web, by creating uniformity - all websites now are chronological, because of one piece of software. But I do handcraft my HTML. I could do anything with it - why do I stick to the plainest, most boring, standard, unimaginative structure possible? I could do anything. Well, if I have the HTML/CSS/JS skills for it (which I don't, to be honest.)

One idea that I have, which will take ages to implement, but would be cool, is to have the blog in the form of an actual garden. So make a little game where the user can move about in a square grid in a garden, interact with different flowers, and each of these tells a story - the entries of the blog.

Until that is implemented though (and it would take ages. I don't know the first thing about JavaScript. Maybe I could write it in C++ using WebAssembly? I think it still requires some JS as glue code, but hopefully not much), I could of course just do the simple thing: get rid of the dates. I could just organise everything as a big list. I think I might do that. If I did that, I should keep the old style as well for phone users or people who don't want to run JS/WASM/etc. I would also want to keep an RSS feed going, so I could have the garden and the regular blog (just a mirror of the garden in more boring form.)

I also need to make it easier for myself to just put short messages up on the website, so I have some medium that isn't a blog post. I was thinking a domain /inconseq, for inconsequential thoughts, but maybe just mix them in with the blog entries.
