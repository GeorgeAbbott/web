Reading Plato

A while ago I bought a book containing the full works of Plato, aptly named Plato: Complete Works , by John M. Cooper. I have still not gotten around to reading it - however, I realise that an understanding of Platonic philosophy is essential to understanding Christian philosophy, and the Metaphysics of Quality , though the Metaphysics of Quality - which I must admit, has a truer feel to me, would say that Platonism is incorrect, as it subjects Quality, that highest nature, to become subordinate to the Forms - or that was my thought, though I have forgotten much, and do not now see how these are related.

As such, I will endeavour to read one book of Plato's per month. This month, September 2023, shall be Euthyphro and if possible Apology - then, I will progress with reading one book a month, until I have read the works of Plato. I will analyse it where I can, if I understand it.

I saw another person who also had a blog doing the same. I will link back to the blog if I find it again. I'm relatively new to the Web 1.0 stuff, so am not keeping a good track of all the different sites I visit...

I will track it here.
