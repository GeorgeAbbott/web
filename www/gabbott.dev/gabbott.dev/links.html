Links

Below are some links to various cool webpages and things I have found.

IndieWeb

  • IndieWeb - Of course.
  • Big List of Personal Sites - Self explanatory.
  • Your World of Text - You can write stuff here. It's super cool!

Blogs

  • parity bit - a blog with various entries - the digital garden is worth checking out.
    • Some Thoughts on the Real World by one who Glimpsed It and Fled.
  • Manuel Moreale's Blog - there is so much good reading on here. I highly recommend it.
  • Alex Murrell's Blog
    • The Age of Average - an insightful blog post about the tendency for everything nowadays to be very... samey.
    • The rest of the entries have a lot to do with advertising, which frankly I could not care less for. Some of them are interesting, but ads just really don't butter my bread.
  • Idiomdrottning - the entries on externalities and copyright are interesting, but the whole page is great.
  • Programming in the 21st Century - A blog about various programming bits.
  • bitbashing.io - A tech blog with a few entries, on C++ and TeX mainly.
  • Manishearth - A blog from a Rustlang developer.
  • arp242.net
  • soc.me - Various programming and language design topics.

Youtubers

  • latintutorial - probably the best channel on Latin grammar.

Programming

  • movfuscator - compiles C into just the mov instruction
  • tcc - The Tiny C Compiler, for quickly compiling C. Maybe good for automatic recompilation of, e.g. dwm?
  • Jack Crenshaw: Let's Build a Compiler - a series teaching how compilers work
  • Linux Daemon How-to - a guide to writing daemons for POSIX systems.
  • The Geometry of Innocent Flesh on the Bone: Return-into-libc without Function Calls - a paper about reinterpreting x86 instructions to create gadgets.

Agriculture

  • Real Seeds - A seed shop with a focus on growing real seeds, without toxins, and saving seed.
  • Charles Dowding - Charles Dowding, the man behind no-dig.

Sustainability

  • omick.net - shelter, food, water, toilets, cooking, etc.
  • Strong Towns - a (granted America-centric) movement about strong urban infrastructure and development.

Philosophy

  • The Hermetic Texts - all major Hermetic texts, and Plotinus's Enneads, all online, and without cost.

Miscellaneous

  • Gastropod: The Golden Spoon - The first episode of the podcast, about spoons, their history, and how they affect flavour. Interesting! I'd never thought of it before, and now I want a golden spoon.
